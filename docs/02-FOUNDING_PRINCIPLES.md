# Aegis Signal
## Founding Principles
### Version: 1.0

> “Technology changes. Markets change. Strategies change. Principles endure.”

---

### Purpose
This document defines the permanent principles that guide every product, engineering, architectural, and business decision within Aegis Signal.
Every contributor—human or AI—must understand and follow these principles.
If a decision conflicts with these principles, the principles take precedence.

---

### Principle 1 — Protect Capital Before Pursuing Profit
The first responsibility of Aegis Signal is not to make money.
It is to avoid unnecessary losses.
Every strategy, feature, and system must prioritize capital preservation before opportunity generation.

---

### Principle 2 — Evidence Over Opinion
Opinions are never executable.
Evidence is.
Every recommendation, signal, score, and alert must be backed by measurable data.
No feature should exist because someone “believes” it works.

---

### Principle 3 — Every Signal Must Be Explainable
A signal without an explanation is not intelligence.
Every signal generated by Aegis Signal must clearly communicate:
* **Why it exists**
* **Which strategy generated it**
* **Which conditions were satisfied**
* **Which risks remain**
* **Why confidence was assigned**

Nothing inside Aegis Signal should behave like a black box.

---

### Principle 4 — Strategies Are Independent
Strategies are plugins.
They are never hard-coded into the platform.
A strategy may be:
* added
* removed
* upgraded
* disabled

without affecting the rest of the system.
The platform survives strategy evolution.

---

### Principle 5 — The Platform Is More Valuable Than Any Strategy
Strategies come and go.
The platform remains.
Engineering effort should always strengthen the platform rather than optimizing a single strategy.

---

### Principle 6 — Configuration Over Code
Whenever possible:
* Users should change configuration.
* Developers should not rewrite code.

Every threshold, indicator period, confidence multiplier, and risk parameter should be configurable unless doing so would compromise correctness or security.

---

### Principle 7 — Modular by Default
Every component must have a single responsibility.
Modules communicate through well-defined interfaces.
Modules never depend directly on internal implementation details of other modules.
* Loose coupling.
* High cohesion.

---

### Principle 8 — Simplicity Is a Feature
Complexity is expensive.
Every new feature increases maintenance cost.
Prefer simple systems that are understandable, testable, and maintainable.

---

### Principle 9 — AI Assists. AI Does Not Decide.
Artificial Intelligence exists to enhance understanding.
Never to replace deterministic logic.
AI may:
* explain
* summarize
* analyze
* detect anomalies
* assist research

AI may never bypass risk rules or deterministic strategy validation.

---

### Principle 10 — Every Decision Must Be Measurable
If success cannot be measured, the feature is unfinished.
Every module must expose metrics.
Every strategy must expose performance.
Every decision must be auditable.

---

### Principle 11 — Build for Scale From Day One
Never build “temporary” architecture.
Even Version 1 should support future growth.
Every major system should be designed with expansion in mind.

---

### Principle 12 — One Source of Truth
Every concept must have exactly one authoritative owner.

**The ownership map lives in [AGENTS.md §2](../AGENTS.md). That table is the
authority that assigns every owner, and it is the single source of truth for
this project.** When two sources disagree, the owner named there wins. When
ownership itself is unclear, AGENTS.md wins.

Examples of owned concepts:
* **Strategy logic** → [06-STRATEGIES.md](06-STRATEGIES.md)
* **Database schema** → `packages/database` Prisma schema
* **API contracts / DTOs / domain enums** → `packages/contracts`
* **Risk rules, leverage, position sizing** → the Risk Engine
* **Architecture decisions** → [adr/](adr/)

Never duplicate business logic — or a type definition — across the system.

---

### Principle 13 — Fail Safely
When uncertainty exists, choose safety.
When data is missing, reject the signal.
When validation fails, stop execution.
Silent failures are unacceptable.

---

### Principle 14 — Continuous Validation
Backtesting creates confidence.
Paper trading creates evidence.
Live trading creates truth.
No strategy earns trust without progressing through these stages.

---

### Principle 15 — Measure the System, Not Just the Market
The platform must continuously evaluate itself.
Questions Aegis Signal should answer include:
* Is this strategy improving?
* Is confidence calibration accurate?
* Is signal quality increasing?
* Is risk increasing?
* Should a strategy be disabled?
* Is the platform healthier today than yesterday?

The platform itself is a measurable system.

---

### Principle 16 — Trust Is Earned Through Transparency
Users should never feel that the platform is hiding information.
Whenever possible, show:
* signal source
* confidence rationale
* risk assessment
* historical performance
* strategy health
* market regime

Transparency builds long-term trust.

---

### Principle 17 — Documentation Is Part of the Product
Code without documentation is incomplete.
Every major component must be documented.
Architecture must explain itself.
Future contributors—including AI agents—should understand the system before modifying it.

---

### Principle 18 — Test Before Trust
Every module must be validated before deployment.
Testing is not optional.
Testing includes:
* unit testing
* integration testing
* backtesting
* paper trading
* regression testing

Only validated software reaches production.

---

### Principle 19 — Security Is a Feature
Security is not an afterthought.
Protect:
* user accounts
* API keys
* trading data
* strategies
* infrastructure
* notifications

Every security decision should assume hostile environments.

---

### Principle 20 — Improve Every Day
Perfection is not the objective.
Continuous improvement is.
Every release should improve at least one of:
* intelligence
* accuracy
* speed
* scalability
* usability
* transparency
* reliability

Small improvements compound into exceptional systems.

---

### Engineering Oath
Every contributor to Aegis Signal commits to building software that is:
* Explainable
* Testable
* Modular
* Observable
* Secure
* Reliable
* Measurable
* Scalable

Shortcuts that compromise these qualities are unacceptable.

---

### The Aegis Standard
Before any feature is accepted into Aegis Signal, it must answer YES to all of the following:
* [ ] Does it increase intelligence?
* [ ] Does it improve the trader’s decision-making?
* [ ] Is it measurable?
* [ ] Is it explainable?
* [ ] Is it testable?
* [ ] Is it secure?
* [ ] Is it scalable?
* [ ] Does it preserve modularity?
* [ ] Does it align with the Product Bible?

If any answer is NO, the feature must be redesigned before implementation.

---

### Closing Statement
Aegis Signal is not built to chase market hype.
It is built to earn trust.
Every line of code, every strategy, every interface, and every decision should move the platform toward one goal:

**Helping traders make better decisions through disciplined, transparent, and measurable market intelligence.**
